# Glossary: BioAI Product Management Terminology

## Terms A–M

**ADMET** – Absorption, Distribution, Metabolism, Excretion, Toxicity; key pharmacological properties evaluated in drug development.

**Alert fatigue** – Clinical phenomenon where excessive/low-value system alerts cause users to systematically ignore or dismiss them, including valid ones.

**AlphaFold** – DeepMind's protein structure prediction model; a landmark BioAI product that transformed structural biology tooling.

**Automation bias** – Tendency for users to over-trust and under-scrutinize AI-generated recommendations, especially under time pressure.

**Calibration** – Property of a model where predicted confidence/probability matches true observed outcome frequency.

**CDS (Clinical Decision Support)** – Software providing clinicians with patient-specific guidance; may or may not be regulated as SaMD depending on FDA criteria.

**CDx (Companion Diagnostic)** – Test required for safe/effective use of a corresponding therapeutic, typically co-developed and co-approved.

**CLIA** – Clinical Laboratory Improvement Amendments; US federal quality standards governing clinical laboratory testing.

**Complementary diagnostic** – Test identifying a biomarker-defined subgroup likely to respond better to therapy, without being mandatory per drug label.

**DMTA cycle** – Design-Make-Test-Analyze; the iterative experimental cycle central to drug discovery, a key framework for BioAI product instrumentation.

**Expected Calibration Error (ECE)** – Metric quantifying the gap between predicted confidence and observed accuracy across confidence bins.

**Explainability** – The degree to which a model's output/reasoning can be understood by a human user; requirements vary substantially by audience (expert scientist vs. clinician vs. patient).

**Foundation model** – Large pretrained model (e.g., protein language models, single-cell foundation models) serving as a base for downstream fine-tuned applications.

**Generative chemistry** – AI methods for generating novel candidate molecules with desired properties.

**Human-in-the-loop** – Product design pattern where AI output requires human review/approval before action, common in high-stakes BioAI applications.

**KOL (Key Opinion Leader)** – Respected domain expert whose endorsement/engagement carries significant credibility with scientific/clinical customers.

**LIMS/ELN** – Laboratory Information Management System / Electronic Lab Notebook; systems recording experimental data, key integration points for BioAI feedback loops.

**MVM (Minimum Viable Model)** – Analogous to MVP; the smallest model validation scope sufficient to test a core product hypothesis.

## Terms N–Z

**Non-device CDS** – FDA-defined category of clinical decision support software exempted from SaMD regulation if it meets four specific criteria (21st Century Cures Act).

**Out-of-distribution (OOD)** – Input data meaningfully different from a model's training distribution, where performance/calibration often degrades unpredictably.

**PCCP (Predetermined Change Control Plan)** – FDA framework allowing pre-authorized future modifications to an AI/ML SaMD without new full submission.

**Pilot (customer pilot)** – Structured evaluation period where a prospective customer validates a BioAI product against their own data before purchase commitment; often the primary sales conversion mechanism in BioAI GTM.

**PLDDT / PAE** – Per-residue confidence metrics (predicted Local Distance Difference Test / Predicted Aligned Error) output by AlphaFold-class structure prediction models.

**Reference customer** – A named customer willing to publicly endorse/discuss their use of a product; carries outsized weight in BioAI sales cycles.

**RUO (Research Use Only)** – Product labeling indicating a tool is not intended for clinical decision-making; subject to less regulatory scrutiny but legally restricted in use.

**SaMD (Software as a Medical Device)** – FDA/international regulatory category for software intended for medical purposes without being part of a hardware device.

**Selection bias (in active learning contexts)** – Bias introduced when only model-recommended candidates are tested, preventing the model from learning from its false negatives.

**Structure-based drug design** – Drug discovery approach using 3D protein structure information to design/optimize candidate molecules.

**Uncertainty quantification (UQ)** – Methods for estimating and communicating a model's confidence/uncertainty in its predictions.

**Wet-lab / Dry-lab** – Wet-lab: physical experimental biology/chemistry work; Dry-lab: computational/in-silico work; the wet-dry lab feedback loop is central to most discovery-stage BioAI products.
